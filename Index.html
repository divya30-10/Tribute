Tribute to Mahatma Gandhi

About Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi was born on October 2, 1869.His full name was Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi. He was born in Porbandar, a coastal town in Gujarat, India.His father, Karamchand Gandhi, was the Diwan (chief minister) of Porbandar. His mother, Putlibai, was deeply religious.Gandhi was greatly influenced by her values and devotion. He married Kasturba Gandhi at the age of 13.Gandhi left for London in 1888 to study law. He enrolled at the Inner Temple to become a barrister.During his stay in England, he tried to adopt Western customs. Eventually, he returned to his simple Indian values.He returned to India in 1891 after completing his studies. He struggled to establish a law practice in Bombay.In 1893, he accepted a legal job in South Africa. This journey changed the course of his life.In South Africa, he faced intense racial discrimination. He was thrown off a train for refusing to move from a “whites-only” compartment.This incident deeply affected him. Gandhi began to fight against racial injustice.He developed his philosophy of Satyagraha (truth-force) in South Africa. He organized the Indian community to resist discriminatory laws.Gandhi spent over 20 years in South Africa. He led several nonviolent protests and civil rights campaigns.He was imprisoned multiple times for his activism. His efforts eventually brought change to some of South Africa's racist policies.In 1915, Gandhi returned to India permanently. He was welcomed as a national hero.


Gandhi believed in ahimsa, or nonviolence.To him, ahimsa was the highest virtue. It meant not hurting others in thought, word, or deed.He also followed the principle of Satya, or truth. He believed truth and love could overcome hatred.Gandhi's method of Satyagraha was based on these ideals. Satyagraha meant passive resistance through love and peace.He believed in moral power rather than physical force. Gandhi practiced what he preached.He led a life of simplicity and discipline. He wore a loincloth and shawl, spun his own cloth, and lived in an ashram.He believed in self-purification and regular fasting. He respected all religions and often read from the Gita, Bible, and Quran.Gandhi fought against social evils like casteism and untouchability. He promoted education for all, especially women and the poor.He supported village industries and economic self-reliance. He disliked industrialization and excessive materialismGandhi believed politics must be guided by ethics. He stressed the unity of means and ends.His philosophy continues to inspire peace movements worldwide. Martin Luther King Jr. adopted his nonviolent methods.Nelson Mandela also cited Gandhi as a major influence. Gandhi's life was a living example of moral courage.He faced criticism but remained firm in his convictions. He saw every person as a child of God.His concept of Sarvodaya focused on uplifting the weakest. Gandhi viewed independence as a means to social reform.He often said, “Be the change you want to see in the world.” His teachings are still relevant in today's troubled world.Gandhi proved that one man can make a difference.


On January 30, 1948, Gandhi was assassinated.He was killed by Nathuram Godse, a Hindu extremist. His death shocked the entire nation and the world.Gandhi died with the words “Hey Ram” on his lips. Millions mourned his passing.He was cremated on the banks of the Yamuna River. His ashes were immersed in holy rivers across India.His death was a loss to humanity. Achievements

  • Mahatma Gandhi's first major movement in India was the Champaran Satyagraha in 1917.

  • Gandhi promoted Swadeshi—use of locally made goods.

  • Gandhi encouraged the spinning of Khadi

  • Gandhi launched the Salt March in 1930.

  • He marched 240 miles from Sabarmati to Dandi.

  • In 1942, he launched the Quit India Movement.

  • He developed and practiced the philosophy of Satyagraha, or "truth-force," which involved peaceful civil disobedience.

  • Gandhi worked tirelessly to abolish untouchability and improve the status of Dalits, whom he called Harijans (Children of God).

  • Gandhi strongly opposed communal violence and always preached Hindu-Muslim unity.

  • Gandhi encouraged women's participation in the freedom movement and believed in their equal rights.
